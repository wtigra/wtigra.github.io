Clinical Trial #I: Implanted Nerve Electrical Stimulation allows to Selectively Restore Hand and Forearm Movements in Patients with a Complete Tetraplegia (#2014-A01752-45)

Ability of a 12 contacts cuff electrode, placed around upper limb nerves, to selectively activate muscles of forearm/hand has been studied. We have shown that it is possible to selectively activate, via current steering, close and distinct muscles, innervated by the same upper limb nerve in individuals with tetraplegia. No adverse effects were reported after the surgery. In particular, medial and radial nerves stimulation in six tetraplegic persons allowed to activate selectively, (muscular contraction scored at 3 or more on the MRC scale), muscles allowing functional:

  • for patient I : wrist flexion,
  • for patient II : thumb extension,
  • for patient III : supination, wrist extension,
  • for patient IV : wrist extension, thumb extension, fingers extension,
  • for patient V : supination, elbow flexion, wrist extension,
  • for patient VI : fingers flexion, thumb flexion, thumb opposition (key and palmar grips were also observed).





For more details about the experimental protocol and results cf. Chapter 5.1 of my PhD thesis (in French) and Implanted Nerve Electrical Stimulation allows to Selectively Restore Hand and Forearm Movements in Patients with a Complete Tetraplegia.
